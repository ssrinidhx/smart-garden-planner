Peanuts

Arachis hypogaea

Category: Legumes

Peanuts are a delicious and healthy snack that is typically grown in warmer climates. However, there are now varieties which will fare well in cooler areas. Surprisingly, peanuts are not actual nuts, but legumes. This makes peanuts more closely related to beans and peas than they are to cashews, almonds, and other nuts. Peanut plants are perennials, though they are typically grown as annuals.

Growing From Seed

To grow peanut plants, you must start with an existing peanut. Carefully shell the peanut and remove the seed(s). Make sure to not damage the delicate shell of the peanut seeds.

Planting Considerations

Peanut blossoms are self-pollinating, so you can plant a single standalone peanut plant, if you’d like. The peanuts themselves will grow underground, so be sure to have loose, well-drained soil at the planting site.

Feeding

Fertilize peanut plants regularly. Do NOT use a high-nitrogen fertilizer as this can kill peanut plants.

Harvest

When the plant yellows, it is just about harvest time. This usually occurs late in the summer. To harvest, dig the entire plant out with a spade. Then, shake off any excess soil, and hang the plant to dry for about a month.

Storage

Peanuts can be eaten raw or roasted. They can also be made into peanut butter. Peanuts can be frozen after blanching.

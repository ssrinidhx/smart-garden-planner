Marigold

Tagetes erecta

Category: Flowers

These bright orange flowers add a splash of color to the garden. Marigolds have the added benefit of being a natural pest deterrent, making them an excellent companion to a plethora of plants. Additionally, if properly cared for, marigolds will bloom all summer long!

Quick Info

Spacing

1/Square

Soil Type

Well-draining, moderately fertile soil

Depth

1/4 inch deep

Sun

Full Sun

Water

1 in/week

Germination

1-2 weeks

Sprout to Maturity

8-10 weeks

Season

Warm

Frost Resistance

Not-Tolerant

Soil pH

Neutral to Slightly Acidic

Growing From Seed

Sow seeds directly in the ground and cover with a thin layer of soil (about 1/8 inch deep). Water thoroughly.

Planting Considerations

Remove faded flowers by pinching them off at the head. This encourages the plant to bloom even more!

Feeding

Marigolds grow well in most soils without any additional amendments. However, if your soil is particularly nutrient-starved, an application of slow-release 5-10-5 fertilizer around planting time may help.

Harvest

Harvest when flowers are fully open but tight center. Petals are edible with a citrus, floral, spicy flavor.

Storage

The flowers can be dried for use in floral arrangements.

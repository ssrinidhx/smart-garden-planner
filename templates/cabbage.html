Cabbage

Brassica oleracea var. capitata

Category: Cole Crops

Cabbage is a hardy, leafy vegetable full of vitamins. It can be difficult to grow; it only likes cool temperatures, and it can be a magnet for several types of pests. By planning your growing season and providing diligent care, you may have two successful crops in one year, both spring and fall. Many varieties are available to suit both your growing conditions and taste preferences.

Planting Considerations

Plant cabbage in an area with well-draining soil.

Feeding

Cabbage is a heavy feeder. For this reason, make sure to mix lots of compost into the soil before planting. Fertilize with a 10-10-10 fertilizer solution two weeks after transplanting. Three weeks after that, fertilize with a nitrogen-rich fertilizer solution.

Harvest

Harvest when heads reach desired size and are firm. This will take around 70 days for most green cabbage varieties. Most early varieties will produce 1-3 pound heads. Cut each cabbage head at its base with a sharp knife. After harvesting, bring inside or put in shade immediately. To get two crops from early cabbage plants, cut the cabbage head out of the plant, leaving the outer leaves and root in the garden. Storage

Cabbage can be stored in the refrigerator for no more than two weeks, wrapped lightly in plastic. Make sure it is dry before storing. In proper root cellar conditions, cabbage will keep for up to 3 months.
